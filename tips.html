How Annika works

A quick guide to text-based nutrition tracking.

How it works

The basics

Each text you send is a standalone request. Annika doesn't do back-and-forth conversation — just tell her what you need in one message. No app to open, no menus to navigate. Text it, done.

Getting started

Log food

Text what you ate. Annika estimates calories, protein, carbs, fat, fiber, and sodium.

1 cup plain nonfat yogurt w 1/4 c blueberries
2 poached eggs, pinch salt, no butter and 2 slices dry wheat toast
Pro tip

Be specific

The more detail you give, the better the estimate. Include quantities, brands, and cooking methods when you can.

1/2 cup blueberries
instead of "some blueberries"
6 oz grilled salmon
instead of "salmon"
Snap a photo

Nutrition labels

Snap a photo of any Nutrition Facts label and send it. Annika reads the label and logs it automatically. Add a caption with the product name for best results.

Want to save a labeled product as a staple? Send the photo, then text "save as staple" or give it a name like "save as my protein bar." Next time just text "my protein bar."

One more thing

Good to know

Annika works best with simple, one-at-a-time requests. She can't edit numbers on something you already logged or pick up a conversation thread — but she's fast. If something's off, just delete it and re-log. Two texts, done.
